About

I am a Research Scientist at Singapore Management University, working on AI-driven wearable healthcare and health informatics. My research spans large-scale clinical data acquisition, biomedical signal analysis, interpretable and personalized AI/ML, edge deployment, and wearable device prototyping.

I received my Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from Zhejiang University in 2024, and was a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (2024–2025), supervised by Prof. Yuanting Zhang (IEEE Life Fellow) and Prof. Ni Zhao. I have authored 17 peer-reviewed publications (7 as first/co-first author).

I am currently interested in foundation models for physiological signals, motion-robust wearable sensing, and translating research prototypes into deployable health systems.

Projects

Unleashing the Potential of PPG for Wearable Healthcare 2025 – Present

Singapore MOE AcRF Tier 2 Project (1M SGD) · Key Researcher. Building PulseLM, the first PPG-to-Text foundation model; CP-PPG, a GAN framework recovering distorted waveforms by 40%; and PPG-Sport, the first bilateral-wrist PPG dataset for high-intensity sports.

Cuffless BP Smartwatch with Multi-Wavelength PPG & ECG 2024 – 2025

Industry–Academic Collaboration (6.02M HKD) · Project Lead. Led a 10+ person team and a large-scale clinical study across 7 hospitals (~7,000 participants), delivering interpretable, lightweight AI for blood pressure monitoring and vascular condition identification.

Reliability Testing of Wearable Smart Products 2017 – 2021

National Key R&D Program (5M CNY) · Key Researcher. Built interpretable BP and core-temperature models from ECG/PPG/ABP; adaptive calibration improved long-term tracking accuracy (SBP +36.6%, DBP +32.3%) and reached 92% CV-risk screening accuracy on Jetson edge prototypes.
